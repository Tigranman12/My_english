# English Learning Plan — 1 Hour Per Day

## Daily Schedule (60 minutes)

| Time       | Activity                        | Duration |
|------------|---------------------------------|----------|
| 0:00–0:15  | Vocabulary (new words + review) | 15 min   |
| 0:15–0:30  | Grammar                         | 15 min   |
| 0:30–0:45  | Listening / Speaking            | 15 min   |
| 0:45–1:00  | Reading / Writing               | 15 min   |

---

## Weekly Focus

| Day       | Focus                          |
|-----------|--------------------------------|
| Monday    | Grammar rules + exercises      |
| Tuesday   | Vocabulary building            |
| Wednesday | Listening (podcasts, videos)   |
| Thursday  | Speaking practice              |
| Friday    | Reading (articles, stories)    |
| Saturday  | Writing (sentences, short text)|
| Sunday    | Review everything from the week|

---

## Monthly Milestones

### Month 1 — Beginner Foundations
- Learn 200 common words
- Master present, past, future tenses
- Introduce yourself in English

### Month 2 — Building Blocks
- Learn 400 words (total)
- Learn questions, negatives, conditionals
- Hold a simple 2-minute conversation

### Month 3 — Intermediate Progress
- Learn 600 words (total)
- Read short English articles with understanding
- Write a short paragraph (5–7 sentences)

### Month 4 — Fluency Building
- Learn 800 words (total)
- Watch a video in English without subtitles
- Write a full page in English

## Recommended Resources

### Vocabulary
- Anki (flashcard app)
- Quizlet

### Grammar
- English Grammar in Use (Raymond Murphy)
- grammarly.com

### Listening
- BBC Learning English (YouTube)
- English with Lucy (YouTube)

### Speaking
- iTalki (find a tutor)
- Speak to yourself (shadowing technique)

### Reading
- News in Levels (newsinlevels.com)
- Simple English Wikipedia

### Writing
- Keep a daily journal in English (1–3 sentences/day)

---

## Tips
- Be consistent — 1 hour every day beats 7 hours once a week
- Don't fear mistakes — mistakes = learning
- Try to think in English, not translate from your native language
- Review old vocabulary regularly, not just new words
