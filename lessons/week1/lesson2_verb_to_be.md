# Lesson 2 — The Verb "To Be"

> **Level:** Beginner | **Time:** 1 hour | **Week:** 1, Day 2

---

## 🎯 Goals
- Understand the verb **to be** (am / is / are)
- Make positive, negative, and question sentences
- Use contractions naturally

---

## 1. What is "To Be"?

**To be** is the most important verb in English. It means:
- **есть / является** (Russian)
- **է** (Armenian)

It changes depending on the subject:

| Subject      | To Be  | Contraction |
|--------------|--------|-------------|
| I            | am     | I'm         |
| You          | are    | You're      |
| He           | is     | He's        |
| She          | is     | She's       |
| It           | is     | It's        |
| We           | are    | We're       |
| They         | are    | They're     |

---

## 2. Positive Sentences ✅

### Structure:
```
Subject + am/is/are + [description]
```

### Examples:
| Sentence                     | Meaning                    |
|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| I am a student.              | Я студент.                 |
| She is a teacher.            | Она учительница.           |
| He is from Russia.           | Он из России.              |
| We are friends.              | Мы друзья.                 |
| It is cold today.            | Сегодня холодно.           |
| They are happy.              | Они счастливы.             |

---

## 3. Negative Sentences ❌

### Structure:
```
Subject + am/is/are + not + [description]
```

| Long form              | Short form (contraction) |
|------------------------|--------------------------|
| I am not tired.        | I'm not tired.           |
| You are not late.      | You aren't late.         |
| He is not at home.     | He isn't at home.        |
| She is not a doctor.   | She isn't a doctor.      |
| We are not ready.      | We aren't ready.         |
| They are not happy.    | They aren't happy.       |

---

## 4. Questions ❓

### Structure:
```
Am/Is/Are + Subject + [description]?
```

| Question              | Short Answer              |
|-----------------------|---------------------------|
| Am I late?            | Yes, you are. / No, you aren't. |
| Are you a student?    | Yes, I am. / No, I'm not. |
| Is he tired?          | Yes, he is. / No, he isn't. |
| Is she from Spain?    | Yes, she is. / No, she isn't. |
| Are they ready?       | Yes, they are. / No, they aren't. |

---

## 5. Key Vocabulary

| Word        | Meaning       | Example                    |
|-------------|---------------|----------------------------|
| student     | студент       | I am a student.            |

---

## 6. Common Mistakes ⚠️

| ❌ Wrong                  | ✅ Correct               |
|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| I is a student.           | I **am** a student.      |
| She am happy.             | She **is** happy.        |
| They is friends.          | They **are** friends.    |
| He are not here.          | He **is** not here.      |

---

## 📝 Summary

| Rule                     | Example                  |
|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| I → **am**               | I am happy.              |
| He/She/It → **is**       | She is a doctor.         |
| You/We/They → **are**    | We are friends.          |
| Negative → add **not**   | He is not tired.         |
| Question → flip order    | Are you ready?           |
