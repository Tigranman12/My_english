# Lesson 4 — Present Simple Tense

> **Level:** Beginner | **Time:** 1 hour | **Week:** 2, Day 1

---

## 🎯 Goals
- Understand when to use Present Simple
- Form positive, negative, and question sentences
- Use frequency adverbs (always, never, sometimes...)

---

## 1. When Do We Use Present Simple?

Use Present Simple for:

| Use                        | Example                            |
|----------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Daily habits & routines    | I wake up at 7 every day.          |
| Facts & general truths     | The sun rises in the east.         |
| Permanent situations       | She lives in Moscow.               |
| Schedules                  | The train leaves at 9 AM.          |

---

## 2. Positive Sentences ✅

### Structure:
```
Subject + verb (base form)
```
> ⚠️ Add **-s** or **-es** after the verb for **he / she / it**

### Examples:

| I / You / We / They        | He / She / It              |
|----------------------------|----------------------------|
| I **work** every day.      | He **works** every day.    |
| They **eat** at 8 AM.      | She **eats** at 8 AM.      |
| We **study** English.      | It **takes** 1 hour.       |
| I **go** to the gym.       | She **goes** to the gym.   |
| They **watch** TV.         | He **watches** TV.         |

### -s / -es rules:
| Verb ends in...  | Add...  | Example                   |
|------------------|---------|---------------------------|
| most verbs       | -s      | work → work**s**          |
| -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -o | -es | watch → watch**es**  |
| consonant + y    | -ies    | study → stud**ies**       |

---

## 3. Negative Sentences ❌

### Structure:
```
Subject + do not (don't) / does not (doesn't) + verb
```

| Subject         | Helper verb     |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| I / You / We / They | **don't**   |
| He / She / It   | **doesn't**     |

### Examples:
| Sentence                           |
|------------------------------------|
| I **don't** like coffee.           |
| She **doesn't** work on weekends.  |
| They **don't** speak French.       |
| He **doesn't** eat meat.           |

> ⚠️ When using **doesn't**, the verb stays in **base form** (no -s):
> - ❌ She doesn't **works**
> - ✅ She doesn't **work**

---

## 4. Questions ❓

### Structure:
```
Do / Does + Subject + verb?
```

| Subject         | Helper verb |
|-----------------|-------------|
| I / You / We / They | **Do** |
| He / She / It   | **Does**    |

### Examples:
| Question                        | Short Answer                     |
|---------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Do** you like music?          | Yes, I do. / No, I don't.        |
| **Does** she work here?         | Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't.|
| **Do** they speak English?      | Yes, they do. / No, they don't.  |
| **Does** he eat breakfast?      | Yes, he does. / No, he doesn't.  |

---

## 5. Frequency Adverbs

These words say **how often** something happens:

```
always → usually → often → sometimes → rarely → never
 100%                                             0%
```

| Adverb    | Meaning        | Example                          |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------------------|
| always    | всегда         | I always brush my teeth.         |
| usually   | обычно         | She usually wakes up at 7.       |
| often     | часто          | They often go out on weekends.   |
| sometimes | иногда         | He sometimes watches movies.     |
| rarely    | редко          | I rarely eat fast food.          |
| never     | никогда        | She never drinks alcohol.        |

> **Position:** Frequency adverbs go **before the main verb** but **after "to be"**:
> - I **always** drink coffee in the morning. ✅
> - She is **always** late. ✅

## ✏️ Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1 — Add -s or leave as is
1. She (work) _______ in a hospital.
2. They (play) _______ football on weekends.
3. He (study) _______ English every day.
4. I (go) _______ to the gym on Mondays.
5. She (watch) _______ TV in the evenings.

### Exercise 2 — Make it negative
1. I like tea. → _____________________________
2. She works on Sundays. → _____________________________
3. They speak German. → _____________________________

### Exercise 3 — Write the question
1. (you / live / in Yerevan?) → _____________________________?
2. (she / study / English?) → _____________________________?
3. (they / work / every day?) → _____________________________?

### Exercise 4 — Describe your day
Write 6 sentences about your daily routine using frequency adverbs:
```
1. I always _______.
2. I usually _______.
3. I sometimes _______.
4. I rarely _______.
5. I never _______.
6. In the evening, I _______.
```

---

## 📝 Summary

| Form      | I / You / We / They   | He / She / It        |
|-----------|-----------------------|----------------------|
| ✅ Positive | I work.             | She work**s**.       |
| ❌ Negative | I **don't** work.   | She **doesn't** work.|
| ❓ Question | **Do** you work?    | **Does** she work?   |
