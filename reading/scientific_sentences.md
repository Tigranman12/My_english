# Scientific Reading — 30 Sentences

> **Purpose:** Reading & vocabulary practice | **Level:** Intermediate

---

## Physics

1. The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately 299,792 kilometers per second.
2. Gravity is a force that attracts two objects with mass toward each other.
3. An atom is the smallest unit of a chemical element that retains its properties.
4. Energy cannot be created or destroyed — it can only be transformed from one form to another.
5. Black holes are regions of space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape.
6. Quantum mechanics describes the behavior of particles at the smallest scales of energy levels.
7. Time passes more slowly in stronger gravitational fields — this is called time dilation.

---

## Psychology

8. The human brain has approximately 86 billion neurons connected by trillions of synapses.
9. Cognitive bias is a systematic error in thinking that affects decisions and judgments.
10. The unconscious mind stores memories, emotions, and desires that we are not aware of.
11. Neuroplasticity is the brain's ability to reorganize itself by forming new neural connections.
12. Stress activates the body's fight-or-flight response, releasing hormones like adrenaline and cortisol.
13. Sleep is essential for memory consolidation — the brain processes information during deep sleep.
14. Emotional intelligence is the ability to understand, manage, and express emotions effectively.

---

## Buddhism

15. Buddhism was founded by Siddhartha Gautama, known as the Buddha, in the 5th century BCE.
16. The Four Noble Truths teach that suffering exists, has a cause, can end, and there is a path to end it.
17. The concept of impermanence in Buddhism states that all things are constantly changing.
18. Mindfulness is the practice of being fully present and aware of the current moment without judgment.
19. Karma refers to the law of cause and effect — every action has a consequence.
20. The Eightfold Path is a guide to ethical living, mental discipline, and wisdom.
21. Meditation trains the mind to focus, reduce stress, and develop greater self-awareness.

---

## Neuroscience

22. The prefrontal cortex is responsible for decision-making, planning, and controlling impulses.
23. Dopamine is a neurotransmitter that plays a key role in motivation, reward, and pleasure.
24. The amygdala processes emotions, especially fear and the response to threats.
25. Humans use all parts of their brain — the myth that we only use 10% is scientifically false.
26. Mirror neurons activate both when we perform an action and when we observe someone else doing it.

---

## Philosophy of Mind

27. Consciousness is one of the greatest unsolved mysteries in science and philosophy.
28. The mind-body problem asks how mental states relate to physical processes in the brain.
29. Some philosophers argue that reality is constructed by the mind, not perceived directly.
30. Free will is the debate over whether humans make truly independent choices or act based on prior causes.

> **Tip:** Read each sentence aloud. Look up any unknown words. Try to explain each sentence in your own words.
